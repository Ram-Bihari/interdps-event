About Fast Fashion

A business strategy known as "fast fashion" focuses on producing clothes quickly and affordably to keep up with shifting fashion trends. It is dependent on a global network of suppliers and manufacturers who create inexpensive clothing that is then offered for sale at a discount. Every few weeks, fashionable clothing is intended to be offered to customers in an effort to increase their purchasing. The ideas of planned obsolescence are used in fast fashion production to make sure that items of clothing survive a few wears before breaking down or going out of style. Fashion waste refers to the large volume of discarded clothing and accessories generated by the fast fashion industry and consumer habits. It includes garments discarded due to short-lived trends, low-quality materials, and overconsumption, contributing to environmental pollution and landfill overflow. Addressing fashion waste is crucial for promoting sustainability and reducing the negative impact of the fashion industry on the environment.
